# Lenovo Yoga Laptop Stand

## The Problem
So basically, I game and do a bunch of computationally expensive tasks on my laptop. While I do have a PC, its shared among family and is sometimes in use so I do all of my work on this laptop. Only issue, my desk has a cloth over it due to my mother forcing me to put one over it. Hence, the built in cooling system that my laptop currently has is a bit lacking and less effective on cloth. This causes a whole slew of issues such as overheating, hotspots on inconvenient spots on my laptop (namely on the lefthand side with the WASD keys, which can get uncomfortably hot), and overall discomfort while using my laptop. I have been using a stack of books/folders (depending on what is on my desk) as a temporary holder to keep the fans exposed to fresh air, but that doesn't work that well and is messy.

## Solution
My solution? To basically engineer a whole laptop stand in the hopes of relieving the issue and improving cooling on my laptop. The idea is to have the ability to put a large fan underneath the hotspot to cool it down considerably, or at the very least improving ventilation while keeping my desk clear. Its also an excuse to have a practical goal when it comes to learning FreeCAD, so may as well knock out two bird with one stone)

## Features

The most recent version of the stand is at (currently) a 15 degree incline with several rails underneath each cross-beam. There, attachments can be designed and swapped out for convenience, such as a fan or a pencil holder. Its also used to keep the cross-beams together, which will be important as all of the parts are separated so that they can fit on my Ender 3 Pro.
